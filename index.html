Our Material

Two ingredients,
endless possibilities

Egg cartons are discarded every single day in our own neighbourhood. Soaked in water, broken into natural pulp, then bound with flour paste — a new material forms that can be shaped freely and dries firm. No synthetic resin, no preservatives, no chemical coating.

At end of life, just dissolve in water or bury in soil. Microbes do the rest — it returns completely to the earth.

How We Make It

Three steps to a natural material

01

Collect & Pulp

Egg cartons are collected from local grocery stores, cafés, and farms. Soaked in water until soft, then hand-torn to separate the fibres. Minimising transport distance is the foundation of our closed loop.

02

Mix & Form

Flour paste (flour + water) is blended into the pulp to add cohesion. The dough can be hand-formed or pressed into moulds. No chemical hardeners — air drying builds all the strength needed.

03

Dry & Finish

After natural air drying, a small amount of perilla oil improves water resistance and durability. No synthetic chemicals are used at any stage — the finished piece remains fully compostable.

Closed Loop System

A loop that starts
and ends locally

RePULP's entire cycle closes within a radius of a few dozen kilometres. Collected nearby, made nearby, sold nearby — and after use, it feeds the same soil it came from.
