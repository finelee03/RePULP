Brand Origin

Billions of eggs are consumed every day. And with them, the egg cartons that cushioned their journey are discarded. Lightweight, workable, everywhere — yet we have long treated this material as simple waste.

RePULP turns that view on its head. Soak egg cartons in water, break them into pulp, blend with flour paste — a traditional natural binder — and you have a new material that can be shaped into anything. No chemical additives. No synthetic resin.

"What we make is not a product — it is the process by which discarded things find new life."

Why Local Collection?

In ESG management, transport carbon is an easily overlooked factor. RePULP minimises the radius for raw material collection. Egg cartons gathered from neighbourhood supermarkets, cafés, and poultry farms become finished products within tens of kilometres — and are consumed in that same area. This is what we call the Closed Loop.

Local businesses that join as collection partners receive incentives based on volume collected, while consumers use products made right where they live. This short footprint saves transport energy and strengthens the local community.

The End Is the Beginning

When a RePULP product reaches the end of its life, the purely plant-based material — free of any synthetic coating — dissolves in water or decomposes in soil completely. Those nutrients become the ground where the next wheat grows, the soil beside the next farm where chickens live. The cycle is complete.

Core Philosophy

Three Beliefs

⊕

Locality

Good material doesn't have to come from far away. The most abundant resource is what gets thrown away closest to home. A short supply chain cuts carbon and circulates the local economy.

◎

Purity

Egg cartons. Water. Flour. That's everything. Without synthetic resin, preservatives, or chemical binders, you can still make something beautiful and strong. What is artificial cannot break down in nature.

♻

Circularity

Every product is designed with disposal in mind. When a RePULP piece reaches the end of its life, it composts or dissolves in water — fully returning to nature. It never ends as waste.

Composting & Decomposition
  • 1

    Local Egg Carton Generation & Collection Agreements

    Collection agreements are established with farms, supermarkets, and cafés within a 50 km radius. Collection partners receive incentives — discount coupons or points based on volume — to sustain ongoing participation.

  • 2

    Collection → Direct Workshop Delivery (Short Route)

    Movement from collection points to the workshop is kept to a minimum. Shorter transport distance directly equals reduced carbon. Local bicycle couriers or scheduled route vehicles are used.

  • 3

    Made Entirely from Natural Ingredients

    Egg carton pulp bonded with flour paste. No chemical hardeners, synthetic coatings, or preservatives. No hazardous chemicals are produced at any stage of the process.

  • 4

    Distribution to Local Consumers

    Products are also consumed within the same local area. Distribution runs through online and local concept-store channels, and a post-use return programme is offered to purchasing customers.

  • 5

    Fully Biodegradable After Use

    Free of chemical substances, products can go straight into a regular compost bin or garden bed. Soil microbes break them down and return them to organic matter. No landfill required.

  • 6

    Soil → Farm → Back to Egg Cartons

    Decomposed organic matter enriches the soil, and from that soil wheat grows and chickens are raised. A true closed loop where materials circulate within the same local area is complete.

ESG Management

Environment · Social · Governance

E
Environment

Environment

Environmental impact is minimised at every stage — production, distribution, and disposal.

  • 100% recycled waste material (egg cartons)
  • Zero synthetic chemicals used
  • Minimal process wastewater (air drying)
  • Fully biodegradable across the product lifecycle
  • Short supply chain reduces transport carbon
S
Social

Social

We pursue a business model that grows alongside the local community.

  • Local collection partner incentive system
  • Collaboration with small farms and independent businesses
  • Regular eco-education workshops
  • Local job creation (collection, making, distribution)
  • Education kit supply to schools and institutions
G
Governance

Governance

We build transparent processes and a sustainable business structure.

  • Annual environmental impact report published
  • Real-time sharing of collection volumes and carbon savings
  • Fair revenue sharing with partners
  • Consumer return programme in operation
  • Full disclosure of all materials and ingredients used
